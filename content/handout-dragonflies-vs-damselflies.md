<Handout id={`h5`} title={`Dragonflies vs. Damselflies Comparison`} imageSrc={`/handouts/dragonflies-vs-damselflies.png`} altText={`Beautiful Bugs! Dragonflies vs. Damselflies! The Helicopter Hunters - Dragonflies are the fastest fliers in the bug world! They can hover, fly backward, and zoom across ponds at lightning speeds to catch mosquitoes in mid-air. Their massive, beautiful eyes wrap around their whole head, giving them amazing vision to spot tiny prey while flying in the bright sunshine. The Delicate Dancers - Damselflies are much slower and flutter gracefully near the water's edge, almost like tiny fairies dancing among the reeds. They have smaller, perfectly round eyes that stick out from the sides of their heads like tiny little beads on a string! Life Underwater - Before they grow wings, both of these amazing bugs start their lives underwater as tiny swimmers called nymphs. They live in ponds and streams for months or even years, breathing through gills and catching tiny aquatic snacks before climbing up a plant stem to transform! Spot the Difference! Dragonfly: Thick, sturdy bodies. When they land to rest, they hold their four strong wings straight out to the sides like a tiny airplane. Damselfly: Super skinny, neon-colored bodies. When they rest, they fold their delicate wings straight back and pressed together over their backs. Explorer's Challenge - Next time you visit a sunny pond or a quiet stream, watch the colorful bugs zipping over the water! Wait for one to land on a cattail or a lily pad. Look closely at how it holds its wings! Is it a zooming dragonfly resting its wings flat, or a dainty damselfly with folded wings? Remember to look with your eyes, not your hands!`} />
